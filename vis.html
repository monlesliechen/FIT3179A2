Australia fire event has always been a big problem.

We collected fire data from Oct 2019 to Jan 2020 and visualize the data to analyse and improve our solutions.

The map below tells us the locations of each fire event and how severe the event is. We can see from the graph that the majority of the fire area is located at the costal area.
You can select a particular time period at the bottom line chart as a time brush, the fire events that happened between this period will reflect on the map and highlight the worst rating in that period. The line chart at the bottom implies that the majority of fire events happened during summer. Especially the day around New Year.
A clearly rise as the temperature goes higher at 06 Nov 2019 and 01 Dec 2019.

These two graphs tell us the distribution of fire incidents during the day. The probability of a fire at night is almost half of that during the day.
Surprisingly, the night did not help us control the fire well. Despite the temperature drop, the fire rating is still high.
